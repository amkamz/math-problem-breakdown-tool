How to break down a word problem

Three steps, in order, for almost any worded problem.

1

State the goal

Fill in "Find ___ when ___." in plain words — e.g. "Find the time when the height is 10." Don't worry about symbols yet.

2

Name the variables

Find the quantities and give each one a letter, with units. Now the same goal reads in symbols: "Find $t$ when $h = 10$."

3

Extract the equations, then solve

Turn each fact into an equation using your variables — one at a time — then work down line by line until it's solved.

Why this order? The goal in words tells you what to hunt for; naming variables gives you the language to say it precisely. The plain-English goal and the symbolic one are the same sentence — watching the blanks rewrite is the point.
